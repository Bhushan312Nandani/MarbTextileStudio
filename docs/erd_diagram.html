2. Flow of Understanding (How Data Moves)

This illustrates the sequential lifecycle of a user interacting with the apparel marketplace.

graph TD A[User Auth
USERS & USER_SESSIONS] --> B[Browse Catalog
CATEGORIES, BRANDS & PRODUCTS] B --> C[View Details & Variants
PRODUCT_VARIANTS & IMAGES] C -.->|Optional| W[Save for Later
WISHLISTS] C --> D[Add Variant to Cart
CARTS & CART_ITEMS] D --> E[Apply Discount & Address
COUPONS & ADDRESSES] E --> F[Checkout / Create Order
ORDERS & ORDER_ITEMS] F --> G[Payment Processing
PAYMENTS] G -->|Success| H[Deduct Stock
INVENTORY_LOGS] H --> I[Dispatch & Delivery
SHIPMENTS] I -.->|Post-Delivery| J[Leave Review or Return
REVIEWS & RETURNS]

Step 1: Identity & Sellers

Data starts at USERS. Roles define access (Customer, Seller, Admin). Sellers manage their store details via SELLER_PROFILES. USER_SESSIONS secures the JWT lifecycle.

Step 2: Apparel Variations

PRODUCTS act as the parent container. The actual physical items exist in PRODUCT_VARIANTS (handling specific Sizes, Colors, and SKUs). PRODUCT_IMAGES supports multiple angles.

Step 3: Cart & Engagement

Users add specific PRODUCT_VARIANTS to CART_ITEMS. They can also save items to WISHLISTS. Stock changes are securely audited in INVENTORY_LOGS.

Step 4: Full Order Lifecycle

Checkout shifts data to ORDERS (applying COUPONS). PAYMENTS verifies funds, SHIPMENTS tracks courier details, and RETURNS handles post-sale refunds.
